The core bet

The individual app ("Chuck in a truck") is public and social. Keep it separate from the business suite; let them meet only at the marketplace. That separation, and the viral growth it unlocks, is what makes this work, and it is the piece we have under-invested in.

The model

Individual · the missing link

A social app for the trades.

The public face of ToolBelt, and the missing link the flywheel needs. It is the overdue upgrade to the mobile experience workers are already stuck with. Mobile-first, with a desktop companion that mirrors it.

  • ●Social by design. Connect, share, refer, invite. Users bring users.
  • ●Many ways in. Web, invite links, QR and SMS funnels feed one fast path to first apply.
  • ●Viral loops = growth. Every user pulls in the next. The supply engine.
  • ●Discover and apply fast. Recommended feed, one-tap apply, track and withdraw.
  • ●Primary activation funnel. Where most users enter, with clear routes into the business suite.
  • ●Simple contacts. A light roster layer, the business Roster pared down for one person.
  • ●Native-ready + desktop companion. The mobile UI is the product; the web mirrors it.
  • ●Post jobs too, capped. Individuals can post work here, with a cap. Post a lot and you are hiring at scale, so we route you to the business suite.
  • ●Free and public. The business suite is the paid upgrade (sales or self-serve, ToolBelt Pro).

The proven model

This is the Indeed model.

We are not inventing this. Indeed runs a mobile-first job-seeker app and a separate employer dashboard, connected by matching. We are adopting a pattern that already works at scale.

Two separate products

A job-seeker app and a separate employer dashboard, connected by matching.

Mobile-first

Native iOS and Android, 100M+ installs, apply-from-your-phone first-class.

The market is mobile

Roughly two in three applications are submitted on mobile.

Sources: Indeed matching + hiring platform, Indeed Employer Dashboard, Indeed mobile app and App Store listing, Appcast mobile-vs-desktop job-search trends.

The flywheel

The marketplace compounds.

The public app acquires users through viral loops; the ERP suite posts the work. Each side feeds the other.

1

Users bring users

Viral loops on a social, mobile-first app grow the worker base.

2

Businesses bring jobs

The ERP suite posts the work, the demand.

3

The loop closes

Apps route into the Roster; hit the posting cap or need to hire at scale, and you upgrade to the paid ERP suite.

The logic: viral loops acquire users cheaply, that supply makes the marketplace valuable, and businesses upgrade to the paid ERP suite to hire from it. Individual-first is what makes the flywheel spin.

Where they meet

The decision

Two paths for the individual.

Same goal: a public, social app that upgrades what workers use today and feeds the marketplace. Two ways to build it.

Recommended

A separate individual product

Its own mobile-first app + desktop companion, on the Indeed model.

Pros
  • Built for the consumer loop
  • Maximum acquisition and viral growth
  • A true social, consumer-grade feel
  • Native-ready
Cons
  • More to build
  • Its own codebase and native pipeline
  • Longer to ship
See the individual prototype ↗
The former path

Minify the business app

Streamline the Next ERP app into a lighter individual experience.

Pros
  • Fastest to stand up
  • One shell to maintain
  • Less new engineering
Cons
  • A consumer app shaped by a business tool
  • Harder to tune the viral loop
  • Lower growth ceiling
