Product vision · internal proposal

Two experiences.
One marketplace.

Individuals and businesses want completely different things from ToolBelt. So we build two purpose-built products, and connect them where it matters: a two-sided marketplace where workers meet work.

A proposal to align on direction, not a finished spec. The prototype and Figma files are idea-sharing artifacts; final requirements and designs will evolve with the business team and other designers on the project.

The core bet

The streamlined individual experience, think "Chuck in a truck," is a separate product from the business app suite. Keeping the two apart, and letting them meet only at the marketplace, is what makes this work.

The model

Different needs. One place they meet.

The two experiences do not mix. They connect only at the marketplace, a two-sided flywheel: businesses bring the jobs, and the streamlined individual experience, with its viral networking loops, brings more users.

Individual

Grow the user base

Individuals and small one-operator companies. Networking and viral loops that pull in more users. Find work, apply, build a profile.

Marketplace

Where they meet (they don't mix)

Jobs flow in from the business side. Users flow in from the individual viral loop. Applications route into the business's Roster. More users attract more businesses, more jobs attract more users.

Business

Vet and hire

Businesses and org admins. CRM-style vetting of contacts in the Roster, labor requests, org, members and permissions, and hiring.

Individual experience · the alignment ask

A mobile-native app, built for the loop.

This is the layer we are seeking alignment on. A standalone consumer product for individuals and small operators, separate from the business suite, on the proven Indeed model: a mobile-native app (iOS + Android) with a desktop companion that mirrors the exact same UI. It is where the networking and viral growth happen, so it leads on mobile.

  • ●Discover and apply, fast. A recommended-work feed, one-tap review-and-send apply, tracking and withdraw.
  • ●Networking + viral loops. Connections and referrals that grow the user base, the supply side of the marketplace.
  • ●Native-ready. The mobile UI is the product the native iOS and Android apps are built on.
  • ●Desktop companion. The web app mirrors the mobile experience, not a separate product.
The proven model

This is the Indeed model.

We are not inventing a structure. Indeed runs the same two-sided split we propose: a mobile-native job-seeker app with a desktop companion, and a separate employer dashboard, connected by matching. Our recommendation is backed by the pattern that already works at scale for labor.

Two separate products

A job-seeker experience and a separate Employer Dashboard, connected by matching and recommendations, not merged into one app.

Mobile-native, mobile-first

Native iOS and Android (100M+ Android installs), with "apply from your phone" as a first-class flow, plus a desktop companion.

The market is mobile

Roughly two in three job applications are submitted on mobile, and about 95% of users reach the internet on a smartphone.

Sources: Indeed matching + hiring platform, Indeed Employer Dashboard, Indeed mobile app and App Store listing, Appcast mobile-vs-desktop job-search trends.

Business capabilities · qualified

The proven suite, folding into the Portal.

The business side is going the Hardie Portal route (next section). These qualified capabilities from our work, Labor Requests, a CRM-style Roster for vetting contacts, and granular org permissions, get integrated into that Portal shell, along with the specific Figma UI and micro-interactions we need. Desktop-first, responsive for the essentials on mobile.

  • ●Roster, CRM-style. Vet, tag, and track contacts and qualifications, the demand side's hiring pipeline.
  • ●Labor requests. A guided create flow with templates, then a rich request detail that posts to the marketplace.
  • ●Org, members, permissions. Scoped, ABAC-style access per resource, built for real teams.
  • ●Responsive. The full suite reflows to mobile so work does not stop when you leave your desk.
Platform strategy

One design system, two device postures.

Individual

Mobile-forward, native-destined

The phone is the primary product. Native iOS and Android builds carry the networking and viral loop. The desktop web app mirrors the exact mobile-forward UI.

Primary: phone · Desktop: mirror
Business

Desktop-first, responsive

The desktop is the primary work surface for org admin, labor requests, and roster. It is responsive so a mobile user can log in and handle the essentials.

Primary: desktop · Mobile: responsive
The flywheel

The marketplace compounds.

Each side feeds the other. The streamlined individual experience is the acquisition engine for the supply side; the business suite creates the demand.

1

Individuals bring users

Viral networking loops on a mobile-forward experience grow the worker base, the supply.

2

Businesses bring jobs

Org admins post labor requests, creating the demand that makes the marketplace worth showing up for.

3

The loop closes

Applications route into the Roster; more users attract more businesses, more jobs attract more users.

The business direction (decided)

The Hardie Portal is the business shell.

The business side is going this route. We accept the Hardie Portal (prototyped by Rob, with the business and product teams) as the shell and chrome, and integrate the qualified items from our PRDs plus the UI and micro-interactions we need from Figma into it, not the Figma shell. It brings an auto-matching engine that auto-pairs candidates to open posts, a geographic view of jobs and pros, and a matches pipeline. The individual "Chuck in a truck" experience stays a separate product.
