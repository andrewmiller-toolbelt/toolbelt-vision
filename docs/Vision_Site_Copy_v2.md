# Vision Site Copy v2 — tightened for execs + product (decision-clear)

Source of truth for ALL landing-page copy (`toolbelt-vision.html`). Code lane: apply verbatim across the page; where it conflicts with copy in the HTML or item notes, this doc wins. Keep all structural/visual specs (reorder, Two-Paths, beam band, hero effect, dark mode, links groups, public/social growth-engine section).

**Editing principles:** fewest words that carry the point. Benefit-first. Stack the pros, show the cons plainly, make the logic obvious so the reader can decide. No em dashes, no run-ons, never "quietly".

**Top talking points for the split (lead with these on the individual side):**
1. The public individual experience is **a social app for the trades**.
2. It runs on **activation funnels** (many ways in) and **viral loops** (users bring users). This is the growth engine.
3. Business is the private, paid counterpart: a full **enterprise ERP suite**, CRM-like with new vetting plus forecasting and gap analysis.

**The two experiences, one line each:** public-facing consumer side = the individual experience, more like a social media app. Business side = a full enterprise ERP suite, CRM-like with new vetting-style functionality and forecasting / gap analysis.

**The logic (the through-line):** the public social app acquires users cheaply through viral loops → that supply makes the marketplace valuable → businesses upgrade to the paid ERP suite to hire from it. Individual-first is what makes the flywheel spin.
